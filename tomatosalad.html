tomato, sage and horseradish salad

this is a delicious salad when the summer and tomatoes are beautiful and in full splendour.

ingredients

  • beautiful tomatoes
  • 100g butter
  • 2tbsp evoo
  • red onion, finely sliced lengthwise - one could use shallots here
  • 3 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • handful of fresh sage leaves
  • fresh horseradish root
  • chopped parsley

what to do

  1. chop your tomatoes into lovely wedges. place in a sieve over a bowl and dress in plenty of flaky salt. Leave these to sit for twenty minutes or so. Maybe longer if you so desire. this breaks the flesh down.
  2. with your sliced onion in a separate bowl, cover with the vinegar, a pinch of sugar and salt. Leave to steep, mildly pickling.
  3. after these two friends have settled, place the tomatoes on a plate. Leave the onions for now. melt your butter and olive oil.
  4. once the butter is melted, add the sage leaves. Fry these until crisp but keep them moving - avoid burning the butter.
  5. pour the butter and sage leaves all over the plated tomatoes.
  6. add the red onion slices over the tomatoes. Using a microplane, grate the fresh horseradish atop, like flurries of snow in winter, followed by a bit of salt and the fresh parsley.

serve with a gorgeous slice of toast. You could eat this in summer with beans; you could add garlic to the butter; you could skip the butter and use olive oil for a vegan friend. You could be very heavy handed with the horseradish. You could even dribble fish sauce atop the tomatoes to break them down, adding an unparalleled umami layer. There is something very beautiful about tomatoes and fish sauce together.
