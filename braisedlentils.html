my braised lentils recipe

this is a very classic lentils forever recipe. It is a recipe of lentils that one could likely eat forever. And here it all began.

ingredients

tick ingredient
200g green lentils
50g butter
1-2tbsp evoo
1 chopped onion
1 chopped carrot
1 chopped celery stick
2 bay leaves
3 sliced cloves of garlic
handful of thyme, rosemary
150ml red wine
a good jug of veg stock
2tbsp wholegrain mustard
2tbsp red wine vinegar
handful fines herbes, chopped - parsley, tarragon, chives, chervil

what to do

  1. wash your lentils very well.
  2. heat your pan over medium heat, melt butter with olive oil. Add onions, carrots, celery & bay with a good pinch of salt. Let these cook together until soft and fragrant, 10 minutes or so.
  3. add the garlic & herb handful. Cook this a few minutes longer.
  4. tumble the lentils in and stir through velvety mirepoix.
  5. as the lentils slightly steam like clams with their damp shells, keep stirring. once the steam eases - add the glass of red wine. Cook this off.
  6. add your stock, stir & leave to stew.
  7. after 20 minutes or once cooked, add mustard, vinegar. Then fines herbes. Serve with aioli or mustard; ive served with aioli, parmesan and agrodolce aubergine & courgette with mint :)

You should certainly season this at every stage. Do not fear the salt with lentils - it is to their benefit.

This is a wonderfully adaptable recipe. You can chop the vegetables in different ways for different experiences. You can use white wine instead of red wine. You can serve with different mustards. It's also lovely with bread. Imagine this as if it were meat; rich, delicious.
